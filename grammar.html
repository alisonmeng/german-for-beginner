Grammar Fundamentals

It's less complex than you think! (Sometimes)


Jump to 1. Gender, 2. Cases, 3. Articles & Pronouns 4. Verbs & Word Order, or 5. Nouns, Plurals & Capitalization

1. Gender (der / die / das)

Key Points Examples
Every noun has a gender: masculine (der), feminine (die), neuter (das). der Tisch (table), die Lampe (lamp), das Auto (car)
Gender affects articles, adjectives, and pronouns.
Always learn the noun with its article.

2. Cases (Nominative, Accusative, Dative, Genitive)

Key Points Examples
Cases show a word’s role (subject, object, possession). Der Hund beißt den Mann. → The dog bites the man (Nom. vs Acc.)
Articles and pronouns change with case.
Word order is flexible because of this system.

3. Articles & Pronouns

Key Points Examples
Definite (the) and indefinite (a) articles vary by case & gender. Ich sehe ihn. (I see him) / Sie gibt ihm das Buch. (She gives him the book)
Pronouns (ich, du, er, sie…) also change by case.
Memorize common patterns (e.g., den → masculine accusative).

4. Verbs &a Word Order

Key Points Examples
Verb usually comes 2nd in main clauses, last in subclauses. Ich gehe heute ins Kino. / Ich habe das gesehen.
Conjugation depends on subject (ich gehe, du gehst…).
In perfect tense, auxiliary verb + past participle go to opposite ends.

5. Nouns, Plurals & Capitalization

Key Points Examples
All nouns are capitalized in German. die Hunde (dogs), das Krankenhaus (hospital = sick + house)
Plurals follow no single rule—learn them with each word.
Compound nouns combine meanings logically.
